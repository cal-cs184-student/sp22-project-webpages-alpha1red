Part V: Shaders

In a shader program, the texture on the cloth and other objects change. We use the vertex shaders on the vertices and the fragment shaders on the fragments. In a vertex shader, we change the position of the vertices, and in a fragment shader, we find a color value, depending on what shader we use, and assign it. Together, we can change how the objects look using the shaders.

In Blinn-Phong, we calculate three components: ambient lighting, diffuse reflection, and specular highlights. In ambient lighting, we add some constant color. It generally is used for shady areas. In diffuse reflection, we get where most of the shading comes from, where light goes. In specular highlights, we look at light from the mirror reflection direction. When added together, we get Blinn-Phong shading.
